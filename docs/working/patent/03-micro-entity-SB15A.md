## Certification of Micro Entity Status (Gross Income Basis)

Under 37 CFR 1.29(a), an applicant certifies micro-entity status
by establishing ALL of the following four conditions. Read each
carefully and confirm it applies to YOU before signing.

### Condition 1 — Small-entity qualification

I qualify as a **small entity** as defined in 37 CFR 1.27.

> A natural person filing individually satisfies this. An LLC with
> fewer than 500 employees that has not assigned, granted,
> conveyed, or licensed any rights in the invention to a non-
> small-entity also qualifies. B-Tree Ventures LLC satisfies this
> on its own account.

- [x] **I confirm I (or my assignee) qualify as a small entity.**

### Condition 2 — Application filing limit

Neither I, nor any joint inventor, nor any entity to which we are
obligated to assign, have been named on more than four previously
filed US patent applications, excluding:
- Provisional applications.
- International applications for which the basic national fee
  was not paid.
- Applications for which I was obligated to assign rights to an
  employer as part of ordinary employment.

- [x] **I confirm my prior-filings count is four or fewer under
      this rule.**

> If uncertain, check the USPTO Public Patent Search at
> [ppubs.uspto.gov](https://ppubs.uspto.gov/) using your name +
> prior employers. The rule excludes obligatory employment-
> related filings, so most individuals comfortably qualify.

### Condition 3 — Gross-income threshold

Neither I, nor any joint inventor, had a gross income exceeding
three times the median household income for the preceding
calendar year (as reported by the US Bureau of the Census) in
the calendar year preceding the year in which the applicable fee
is being paid.

**For a 2026 filing, the applicable threshold is based on 2025
median household income.** As of the drafting of this template,
the 2025 gross-income ceiling per USPTO is **$223,740 USD**.
USPTO publishes the current number at
[uspto.gov/patents/laws/micro-entity-status](https://www.uspto.gov/patents/laws/micro-entity-status)
— confirm the current number immediately before filing.

- [x] **I confirm my gross income in 2025 did not exceed the
      published threshold** ($223,740 or current published value).

### Condition 4 — Assignment-income threshold

I have not assigned, granted, conveyed, or licensed (and am not
obligated to assign, grant, convey, or license) any ownership
interest in the application to an entity whose gross income in
the preceding calendar year exceeded three times the median
household income.

> B-Tree Ventures LLC, as the intended assignee, must satisfy this
> condition. If B-Tree Ventures' 2025 gross income is below the
> threshold (very likely at a pre-revenue / early-revenue stage),
> this is met.

- [x] **I confirm no ownership interest has been assigned/
      licensed to a non-qualifying entity.**

## What if any condition is NOT satisfied?

If ANY of the four conditions above does not apply to you:

- **Do not sign this form.** Filing a false micro-entity
  certification is itself a filing offense.
- Use **small-entity status** instead (SB/05) if you qualify.
  Small-entity filing fee is $150 (still a bargain).
- Or use **standard entity status** — $320 filing fee. Still
  much cheaper than attorney time.

The most common reason someone who thinks they qualify as a
micro entity actually doesn't: they forgot to exclude employment-related filings from their four-prior-applications count. Read
Condition 2 carefully.

---

## What happens if the certification turns out to be wrong

USPTO guidance (MPEP 509.04): a good-faith error can be corrected
by filing a notification of change of status and paying the
difference in fees. **Willful false certification can invalidate
the patent.** When in doubt, use small entity instead; the savings
aren't worth the risk.
