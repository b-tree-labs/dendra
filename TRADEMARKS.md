these marks. Trademark rights are separate from copyright and
patent rights.

## Fair use — no permission needed

You may use the POSTRULE name and the `@ml_switch` decorator name
in the following ways without contacting us:

- **Describing your project's use of Postrule.** Example: "Our
  ticket triage service uses Postrule for graduated rule-to-ML
  migration." Naming the library as a dependency is descriptive
  use (nominative fair use).
- **Citing the paper or the benchmarks in academic or
  educational contexts.** No license required for scholarly
  citation.
- **Writing blog posts, tutorials, or talks about Postrule.**
  Use the name as a noun ("Postrule supports X") without treating
  the mark as generic ("our postrule"). Avoid suggesting official
  endorsement.
- **Interoperability statements.** "Compatible with Postrule
  v1.0+" is fine. "Postrule Certified" or "Powered by Postrule"
  requires a license.
- **Bug reports, support questions, and contributions.**
  Referring to the project by name is always fine.

## Uses that require a commercial trademark license

Contact `trademarks@b-treeventures.com` before:

- **Selling goods or services under a name incorporating
  "Postrule" or a confusingly similar variant.**
- **Running a website, SaaS, or hosted offering titled "Postrule
  Something" or "Something Postrule".**
- **Producing merchandise** (T-shirts, stickers, mugs) featuring
  the POSTRULE word mark or logo for sale.
- **Representing your product as "Postrule Certified",
  "Postrule Approved", "Official Postrule Partner", or any variant
  implying quality assurance or partnership.**
- **Registering domains, social-media handles, or package names
  that begin with "postrule-" for commercial use.** Non-commercial
  fan sites and descriptive community handles are fine.

## The `@ml_switch` decorator and API names

Postrule's decorator identifier (`@ml_switch`) and other public
API names are part of the library's source, which ships under
Apache 2.0 (client SDK) or BSL 1.1 (operated components).
Copying those identifiers as part of normal library use
(importing, wrapping, extending) is covered by the license, not
by trademark.

If your fork re-exports `@ml_switch` with different semantics in
a way likely to confuse Postrule users, contact us first —
trademark law covers naming confusion even where copyright does
not.

## Logo and visual identity

The Postrule wordmark and any associated logos under `brand/logo/`
are governed by the same rules as the word mark: fair use in
descriptive references is fine; use implying endorsement or
partnership requires a license.

## Reporting misuse

If you see the POSTRULE name or a B-Tree Labs brand used in a way
that seems to imply endorsement or that could confuse users,
please email `trademarks@b-treeventures.com`. Low-severity
misuse is usually resolved with a polite note asking for
updated wording.
