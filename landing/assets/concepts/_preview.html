Dendra — brand mark concepts (round 2)

Round 1 shipped four concepts (Dendrite / Ascend / Curve / Gate). Ben's feedback: "A looks like a Y and the asymmetry is something I'm still trying to process; B appeals; C conceptually best but least favorite image; D has subtle power." Round 2 applies that feedback to each concept with orientation + execution variants, then hands the full set to three AI design critics for assessment.

A · Dendrite

Name etymology — the tree of decisions.

Round-1 feedback Likes the idea, but the Y-shape asymmetry (accent on one branch) is hard to process. Round 2 addresses symmetry (accent at the node), breaks the Y-literal-read (compound canopy), and offers an inverted orientation with visible roots.
A1 · original Asymmetric Y. Accent on right-branch tip. Reads as "one graduation earned."
A2 · symmetric Same Y, but the accent moves into the split node. Perfectly mirrored. "The graduation point IS the ember."
A3 · canopy Two-level branching (4 terminal tips) — less Y-literal, more tree-literal. Accent at center node.
A4 · rooted Trunk rises from a three-root splay. No canopy — the graduation is the ascent itself, accent at the top.

B · Ascend

Lifecycle as shape — six phases, literally.

Round-1 feedback Appeals. Round 2 tries denser rhythm (B2) and an architectural stack that drops the staircase reading (B3).
B1 · original Six staircase steps rising from the rule floor, top step accent.
B2 · compact Tighter rhythm, fills the central region more fully. Reads as a single dense object rather than scattered steps.
B3 · stack Six horizontal bars at the same x-position — pure vertical stack. Reads as "foundational strata," not as motion. More architectural.

C · Curve

Measurement as mark — Figure 1 collapsed.

Round-1 feedback Conceptually the favorite, visually the least. Round 2 attempts three stronger executions: minimal (one dot), tick-marks (ruler-like), and step-function (curve + ascend hybrid).
C1 · original Curve + 5 checkpoint dots + rule floor. Last dot accent.
C2 · minimal Stripped to one curve + one terminal accent dot + rule floor. Single confident gesture.
C3 · tick marks Checkpoints as vertical tick marks crossing the curve. Reads as a measurement ruler — more "instrumentation."
C4 · step-function Curve rendered as a stepped staircase. Discrete phase advances along a rising path — bridges Curve and Ascend.

D · Gate

Theorem as architecture — the threshold crossed.

Round-1 feedback "Cool and has a subtle power." Round 2 tries two small strengthenings: rising-through (dynamic) and open-threshold (minimalist).
D1 · original Floor + two posts + lintel + accent tick rising through the center.
D2 · rising Accent starts below the floor, parts the floor, rises through the gate. Dynamic and directional.
D3 · open threshold Drop the lintel. Two posts on the floor, accent rising between. More gesture, less architecture.
